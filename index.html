About me

Hello!
I am a Ph.D. student in Information Science at Cornell University advised by Prof. Andrea Stevenson Won and Prof. François Guimbretière. My research is to explore the potential of Extended Reality for collaboration and analysis tools.

I have been developing VR/AR systems to support remote collaboration and analyzing nonverbal behaviors in educational contexts. Furthermore, I have been working on investigating the influence of robotic or virtual embodiment on physical and cognitive abilities. As inspiring HCI researcher, my research goal is to investigate how Extended Reality can facilitate interaction between humans and robots, connecting these interactions from virtual space to real space.

Prior to joining Cornell, I worked as a Research Assistant at the AI & Robot Institute at KIST (Korea Institute of Science and Technology). I earned my Master's degree in Culture Technology from KAIST in 2019, and my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Ewha Womans University in 2017.
